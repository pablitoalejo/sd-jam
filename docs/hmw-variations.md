# How Might We Make Thinking Visible?

10 variations grounded in our research synthesis, each targeting a specific audience and outcome.

---

1. **How might we give introverted team members a low-stakes way to sketch a half-formed idea before verbal discussion begins** so that they can put their thinking into the room without waiting for a pause in conversation?
*Grounded in Kevin's quiet brainstorming technique and Sergio's finding that "alignment didn't really happen" when only loud voices were heard.*

2. **How might we surface live definition gaps for cross-functional teams using shared jargon** so that they can catch a word like "alignment" or "system" meaning two different things before a decision gets framed around it?
*Grounded in Bianca's quote: "When we started digging in, we realized people had radically different thoughts as to what we mean by alignment."*

3. **How might we give first-time jam participants a visual map of the session's structure and vocabulary before the work starts** so that they can contribute ideas rather than spend cognitive bandwidth decoding what's happening around them?
*Grounded in Preston's need for "context of the jam before going into sessions" and Bianca's quote about quelling the questions that cause anxiety.*

4. **How might we create a persistent visual thread for remote collaborators joining a design sprint mid-session** so that they can trace how a problem statement evolved before they arrived and step in without derailing the group?
*Grounded in Brandon's echoing and mirroring technique — the idea of "conversational memory" so the group can reorient.*

5. **How might we give junior contributors in hierarchical organizations a structured, anonymous channel to flag a dissenting view** so that the group hears a challenge to the VP's direction without putting one person visibly at risk?
*Grounded in Sergio's quote: "They're agreeing because the VP says something. There's a power dynamic in the room that's not being resolved."*

6. **How might we help facilitators running cross-functional teams make implicit group norms visible to the whole room** so that a toxic dynamic — like a post-meeting Slack thread mocking someone's idea — can be named and interrupted before the next session?
*Grounded in Sergio's observation: "Even if we say, please speak up, there's an implicit incentive not to."*

7. **How might we give cognitively depleted participants arriving after a full work week a low-effort, tactile way to contribute** so that they can put something meaningful into the room without being asked to think at a level they cannot currently access?
*Grounded in Brandon's observation about "dealing with people at partial capacity" and the need to be "generous and gentle in that regard."*

8. **How might we help non-native English speakers in design sprints translate a concept they can draw but not yet word** so that their idea enters the group's shared field before language becomes the bottleneck?
*Grounded in Kevin's quote: "Visuals help a lot — when people can look at it and absorb it at their own pace, without having to hear words that can be misinterpreted."*

9. **How might we build a real-time capture layer for pollinators moving between working groups** so that a connection one team surfaces can reach another team while the insight is still alive, not after the session ends?
*Grounded in Brandon's pollinator concept and his pain point: "The idea dies as soon as you leave it — so why don't you capture it as it's happening?"*

10. **How might we help experienced facilitators show a group the moment false consensus formed — the exact exchange where the box got checked** so that the team can revisit the actual disagreement rather than leaving in swirl?
*Grounded in Sergio's quote: "We check the box, yeah. We walk away thinking we agree, and nobody has really agreed. Then you're in swirl."*
